About Liga Latina Unida

Liga Latina Unida aims to help bring basketball players together to enjoy a friendly and competive environment against other players. The league has been operatings for a many years now. The league first started playing in outside courts at Sepulveda Recreation Center in Panorama City here in the San Fernando Valley. Now the league has grown to operate in 3 gym locations and has a few divisions for all levels of play. Check out our contact section to find out how to register a team.




Divisions

Division 1

Division 1 is designed to promote the most ELITE competition. Open to pro and collegiate ball players featuring no height limitations, this division gives you the opportunity to play against the best players around. If a challenge is what you're looking for, this is your division.


Division 2 A

Division 2 A is a designated division for teams and players of mid level skill level that want to compete in a competitive league, with some height limitations without giving up any level of competition. Mid level of play is expected from teams competing in this division, ready?


Division 2 B

Division 2 B is a designated division for teams and players of amateur skill level that want to compete in a controlled height atmosphere, without giving up any level of competition. High level of play is expected from teams competing in this tier, ready?


Division 3

Division 3 is designated for teams and players of any descent that want to compete in a controlled height atmosphere, in a more recreational and fun type atmosphere. Different tier levels may be enforced based on number of teams.






League Rules

General Rules

  • All participants must be responsible for their own medical insurance. The HUB League and it's owners will not be held responsible for any injuries sustained during games at any gym or around gym premises.
  • All players must read, sign roster sheets, and if necessary provide I.D. before playing in any game
  • 2 Player limit per roster.
  • Each player must play in 1 of the first 3 scheduled games in order to be part of the final roster and participate during the rest of the season.
  • Each player must participate in 4 of the 8 games scheduled in order to participate in the playoffs. No Exceptions.
  • League officials reserve the right to place teams in divisions that fit the league standards for competition.
  • Game fees ($40) must be paid in full before the tip-off of every game. CASH ONLY!
  • Each team will be allowed a 10 minute grace period after scheduled game time has passed. Once the 10 minute grace period has passed the team will assume a forfeit and have to pay the $70 forfeit bond ($35 for your game fee + $35 for the game fee of the team that showed up)
  • Each team must have at least 4 players to start a game, with the 5th player showing up before the start of the 2nd half. If the 5th player has not appeared by the start of the 2nd half, the game will become a forfeit and the forfeiting team will have to pay the $70 forfeit bond.
  • Fighting will result in automatic ejection of players involved. In addition, all players involved in a fight will be banned from the league. NO EXCEPTIONS The league committee will review all incidences. Players will only be reinstated with approval of league officials.
  • No jewelry will be allowed while playing. Anyone playing jewelry will receive a technical foul.

Game Play

  • We two 20 minute periods. Stop clock enforced during the last 2 minutes of second period.
  • Overtime periods will last 2 minutes ( 2 overtime periods maximum)
  • If a game has not been decided after 2 overtime periods a Sudden Death period will commence. First point from either team ends the game.
  • Each team is allowed 2 timeouts per period and 1 timeout per overtime.
  • Timeout cannot be carried into overtime
  • Each player is allowed 5 personal fouls.
  • Technical fouls count as personal fouls as well as team fouls.
  • Two technical foul constitutes an ejection from the game and the facility.
  • Teams will be in the bonus after six (7) team fouls, they will be awarded with a 1 and 1. After ten (10) team fouls the team will be awarded two (2) free throws.
  • Substitutes must report to scorer’s table and wait to be called into the game by officials. Walking onto the court before being called in will result in a technical foul on that player.

Uniforms

  • In order to keep the professional look of the league, The Liga Latina enforces that all participating teams must wear identical uniforms.
  • You can either provide your own, or we may direct you to where you may have some made for your team.
  • Any mismatched uniforms will receive a technical foul
  • Any duplicate numbers will garner a technical foul
  • Any jersey with no number will garner a technical foul

Code of Conduct

  • Captains will be held accountable for the conduct of himself, his team and the team's fans.
  • Captains are responsible for all communication and grievances.
  • Captains are considered the sole representative of the team.
  • Captains are responsible to see that the team is informed of the "Players code of conduct", that lists infractions and penalties associated with each code.
  • Any person ejected from a game - whether before, during or after a game, will automatically be suspended from the next scheduled game as well as placed on probation for the remainder of the season.
  • A second ejection will result in suspension from further play in the league.
  • At no time shall any player, coach, manager:
    1. Lay a hand upon, push, shove, strike or threaten to strike any staff, official player or spectator.
    2. Refuse to abide by an official's decision.
    3. Verbally abuse any staff, official, player or spectator.
    4. Demonstrate unsportsman-like conduct by throwing foreign object(s) or by any other forceful action.
    5. Be guilty of unnecessary rough tactics against any opposing player.
    6. Use profane, obscene, or vulgar language, nor use hand gestures at any time.
    7. Appear on site in an intoxicated manner.

Penalties

  • The degree of infraction will determine penalties associated with it.
  • Minimum penalty will be a warning, maximum penalty may be up to a 12 month suspension from league play.
  • Players with ongoing ejections or ejected for fighting will be required to submit in writing a letter including his/her intent for continued play in the adult sports program. This letter must be signed by both the player and manager for which he/she plays prior to reinstatement.
